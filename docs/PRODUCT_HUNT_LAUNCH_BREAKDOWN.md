### 2. **Product Description (300-500 words)**
**Structure (5-paragraph framework):**

#### Paragraph 1: The Hook (Problem + Promise)
**Pattern:** "You know that feeling when [relatable pain]? We built [product] to fix that."

**Psychology:**
- **Pain recognition** - Validates their struggle
- **Pattern interrupt** - Breaks the scroll
- **Implied transformation** - Hints at solution without overselling

**Example:**
```

but you're drowning in strategy docs, competitor research, and
endless rewrites? We built aiCMO to fix that.
```

#### Paragraph 2: The Problem (Twist the Knife)
**Pattern:** Make the problem feel urgent and expensive

**Psychology:**
- **Loss aversion** - People avoid losses more than they seek gains
- **Quantification** - Numbers make pain concrete
- **Status quo bias** - Show cost of inaction

**Example:**
```

‚Ä¢ Hiring a content team costs ¬£5K+/month
‚Ä¢ Freelancers are inconsistent and need constant management

#### Paragraph 3: The Solution (Your Unique Approach)
**Pattern:** "Instead of [old way], we [new way]"

**Psychology:**
- **Contrast effect** - Highlight your differentiation
- **Simplicity** - Show how easy it is
- **Authority** - Reference your unique insight/tech

**Example:**
```

#### Paragraph 4: How It Works (3-Step Process)
**Pattern:** Show simplicity in 3 steps

**Psychology:**
- **Rule of three** - Brains love patterns of three
- **Process visualization** - Reduces perceived effort
- **Time compression** - Show speed

**Example:**

1. Enter your website URL (2 minutes)
2. aiCMO analyzes your brand, market, and competitors (automatic)
